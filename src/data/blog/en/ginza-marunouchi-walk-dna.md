To many visitors, Ginza and Marunouchi are remembered simply as 'shopping paradise' and 'concrete jungle.' However, through the lens of an investor, walking this path is like traversing a vast museum of Japan's national credit and asset history.

As of early 2026, from the Yamano Music site in Ginza—which has held the title of Japan’s most expensive land for 20 consecutive years—to the Otemachi district where the 'Torch Tower' (set to be Japan's tallest building) is rising, we introduce a walking route that defines Tokyo’s DNA.

---

<p class="post-section-heading">1. Ginza: Immutable Value, the Pinnacle of Luxury</p>

Ginza demonstrates the power of 'classic' over 'trendy.'

*   **Benchmark Value**: In 2026, land near the Ginza 4-chome intersection continues to record the highest prices in Japan. This is not just a number but a testament to a robust fundamental market driven by both domestic demand and global tourism.
*   **A Parade of Flagship Stores**: Global luxury brands establish themselves here not just for profit but for 'brand prestige.' In 2026, Ginza has become a testing ground for high-end retail that blends digital technology with tactile offline experiences.

---

<p class="post-section-heading">2. Marunouchi & Otemachi: The Skyline Building Japan's Future</p>

Stretching west of Tokyo Station, Marunouchi is the command center of the Japanese economy.

*   **Historic Red Brick meets Modern Towers**: The classical exterior of Tokyo Station surrounded by sleek office towers visualizes the unique Japanese philosophy of 'innovation through tradition.'
*   **The Tokyo Torch Project**: The ongoing construction of the ~390m 'Torch Tower' (aiming for 2028 completion) showcases how the Marunouchi-Otemachi district is evolving beyond a simple office zone into a global pinnacle for business and tourism.

<p class="post-section-heading">4. Conclusion: "Walking Becomes Investment Insight"</p>

Passing through Ginza’s Chuo-dori and walking along the tree-lined Marunouchi Naka-dori, you can personally feel why Tokyo is evaluated as one of the world's premier safe-haven asset locations. In 2026, this path is not just a beautiful scenery but a profound demonstration of the Japanese economy's resilience and confidence in the future.

Investors, when you visit Tokyo, take this 1.5km walk. The future of Japanese real estate and business lies at the end of your footsteps.
